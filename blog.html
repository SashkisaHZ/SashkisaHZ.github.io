From MBO to HBO-ICT: Taking My Tech Skills to the Next Level at HZ university of applied Sciences

Read more...

Choosing to enroll in the HBO-ICT bachelor's program at HZ University of Applied Sciences in Middelburg was a natural progression for me. I hold an MBO diploma as a Computer Technician from Bulgaria, where I first discovered my passion for understanding and working with technology. My curiosity has always driven me to explore how things work, from dismantling gadgets to learning how to build and program them. The HBO-ICT program at HZ UAS offers a comprehensive approach to technology and its applications in the modern world. The curriculum's focus on practical skills, combined with a strong theoretical foundation, aligns perfectly with my goal of becoming an IT professional who is not only proficient in current technologies but also adaptable to future advancements. Moreover, the program's emphasis on innovation and problem-solving matches my desire to engage in projects that challenge me to think critically and creatively. Middelburg, with its vibrant student community and supportive learning environment, is the ideal place for me to develop my skills further. I am excited about the opportunities this program provides for collaboration, real-world experience, and personal growth. My journey into the world of technology has only just begun, and I am eager to see where it will take me next at HZ university.

Learning to Code

September 3, 2024 by Alexander Atanasov

My Journey into Programming: From Web Design to Coding Languages

Read more...

Programming has always intrigued me—it's like solving a complex puzzle, where each piece fits perfectly to create something functional and meaningful. My journey into programming began with the basics of HTML and CSS, which I consider to be my foundational tools. These languages opened the door to web development, allowing me to design and structure web pages. I have a solid understanding of HTML and CSS, which has enabled me to create well-structured, responsive websites with a clean design. As I became more comfortable with web development, I decided to expand my skills beyond front-end design and dive into more complex programming languages. This led me to explore C#, C++, and Python, where I have gained basic experience. Each of these languages has its unique strengths, and my experience with them has been a mix of self-learning and practical application.
C# was my first step into more advanced programming. I started with simple console applications, gradually moving on to more complex tasks like developing basic algorithms and understanding object-oriented programming concepts. C# has been particularly interesting because of its versatility and its use in developing various types of applications, from desktop software to games using Unity.
With C++, I ventured further into understanding how programming interacts with hardware. Although my experience is still at a basic level, I've grasped the capabilities it provides. This language has taught me to think more critically about how code is executed behind the scenes, making me appreciate the efficiency and optimisation aspects of programming.
As for Python, my experience is more limited. I've primarily used it to create basic "bots" for music, which was a fun way to apply my programming knowledge in a practical context. However, I recognize that I still have much to learn when it comes to Python's full capabilities.
My experience with these languages, although still developing, has been invaluable in shaping my understanding of programming. Each one has taught me different approaches to solving problems and how to think logically and creatively. Moving forward, I plan to continue building my skills in these languages while exploring new ones and expanding my knowledge of software development.

ICT nowadays

July 25, 2023 by Alexander Atanasov

Breaking Through the Digital Ceiling: ICT Skills and Labour Market Opportunities

Read more...

ICT skills are known to be distributed unevenly across the population. In the early 2000s numerous studies pointed out that there exists a big digital divide between the genders. Women tended to have less access to the internet, and if they did have access, they had fewer occasions to use it and tended to use it for different reasons than males. According to Desjardins, the gap between access and use has narrowed significantly over time, at least in developed economies. Nowadays, digital technologies generate opportunities for those who possess the level of ICT skills demanded by the labour market, but they limit the options for those who do not possess these skills or have failed to upgrade them accordingly. As the pace of innovation in ICT technologies accelerates and their diffusion increases, workers are coming under growing pressure to adjust continuously. Consequently, we observe that job changes between different occupational groups are rather rare, which supports the concepts of task-specific and occupation-specific human capital. Furthermore, individuals who possess strong ICT skills tend to have greater opportunities in the labour market. ICT skills increase the probability to progress towards a new job, and especially towards jobs which are characterised by ICT-intensive tasks. These jobs tend to be better paid and are more likely to offer permanent contracts.
